Methodology & Confidence Intervals

Understanding how our temporal-weighted model works and computes salary ranges

Temporal Weighting

Our model uses exponential temporal weighting: w(t) = e^(-λ(T-t)) where more recent years receive higher importance. 2024 data has 2.9x more influence than 2017 data, ensuring predictions reflect current market conditions.

Random Forest Model

We use a Random Forest with 100 trees, each trained on different subsets of the data with temporal weights. This ensemble approach provides robust predictions and enables uncertainty quantification through tree variance.

Confidence Intervals

Addressing the Professor's Question: Our salary ranges are 95% confidence intervals computed from Random Forest tree prediction variance:

CI = μ ± (1.96 × σ_trees / √100)

Where μ is the mean prediction across trees and σ_trees is the standard deviation of individual tree predictions.

Three-Model Validation

We validated our approach using three models:
• Baseline (2024 Test): Traditional training on 2017-2023, testing on 2024
• Temporal-Weighted: Same split with exponential weighting
• Comprehensive: Traditional cross-validation on all years

About This Calculator

Built by data science students using advanced machine learning techniques

Research Project

This calculator is the result of a comprehensive data science research project conducted by Hosni Abomokh and Itay Pizanty under the supervision of Prof. Mario in the Data Science Lab.

Methodology Innovation

Our key innovation is the implementation of temporal weighting in salary prediction models. Unlike traditional approaches that treat all historical data equally, our method assigns higher importance to recent years, reflecting the rapid pace of change in the technology sector.

Data Source

The model is trained on 268,935 developer responses from the Stack Overflow Developer Survey spanning 2017-2024. This represents one of the largest and most comprehensive datasets for developer compensation analysis.

Academic Rigor

Our approach includes:
• Three-model validation framework
• Confidence intervals based on Random Forest tree variance
• Comprehensive feature engineering and selection
• Temporal weighting with exponential decay (λ = 0.15)
